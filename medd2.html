POPULAR TOURIST SPOTS IN BAGUIO

Discover iconic destinations across the City of Pines‚Äîfrom serene parks to sweeping mountain views. Tap a card to see more and get directions.


  • Burnham Park

    The heart of Baguio City, famous for its tranquil lagoon, swan boats, rose garden, and open spaces perfect for picnics and leisurely strolls.

  • Mines View Park

    A classic vantage point offering panoramic views of Benguet‚Äôs mountain ranges and former mining townscapes‚Äîbest at sunrise.

  • The Mansion

    The official summer residence of the Philippine President, admired for its historic gates, European-inspired architecture, and gardens.

  • Wright Park

    Known for the Pool of Pines, horseback riding, and a cool canopy of towering trees leading to The Mansion‚Äôs famed gates.

  • Camp John Hay

    A sprawling pine estate with nature trails, coffee nooks, and heritage spots‚Äîideal for cycling, golfing, or laid‚Äëback forest walks.

  • BenCab Museum

    A premier art destination by National Artist Benedicto Cabrera, featuring Cordilleran culture, modern works, and a serene farm garden.

  • Igorot Stone Kingdom

    A relatively new cultural theme park built entirely of stone, showcasing Igorot culture and folklore. Famous for its castle-like structures, multi-tiered walls, and stunning viewpoints inspired by the Cordillera rice terraces.

  • Tam-awan Village

    This Garden in the Sky is an artists haven featuring reconstructed, authentic Ifugao and Kalinga huts alongside art galleries and cultural workshops.

  • Baguio Botanical Garden

    A tranquil, lush park offering a blend of nature and culture, famous for its towering pine trees, various themed gardens, and the Cordillera Village with traditional huts and the Japanese Tunnel.

  • Bamboo Educational Demo Farm

    A serene "Bamboo Sanctuary" featuring lush groves and tranquil trails, dedicated to educating the public on over 100 species of bamboo and sustainable use.

  • Lion's Head

    This iconic 40-foot landmark carved into a limestone boulder serves as the symbolic gateway to Baguio City along Kennon Road.

  • Dragon Treasure Castle

    A new, fantasy-themed attraction in Baguio featuring medieval-inspired stone architecture. It offers stunning panoramic views, including the sea of clouds and the Kissing Rock limestone formation.

Weather at a Glance

Cool year-round ‚Ä¢ Elevation: ~1,470 m
Dry Season

Nov‚ÄìMay, pleasant days (18‚Äì24¬∞C) and cool nights (12‚Äì16¬∞C). Bring a light jacket.

Rainy Season

Jun‚ÄìOct, frequent showers. Pack a compact umbrella, rain jacket, and quick-dry shoes.

What to Wear

Layer up. Mornings/evenings can be chilly. Sunblock is still useful‚ÄîUV is higher at altitude.

Average temps range from 12‚Äì24¬∞C. Coldest months: December‚ÄìFebruary. Warmest: April‚ÄìMay (but still cooler than lowlands).

Fog can roll in quickly. If driving, use low beams and keep safe distances.

Quick Packing Notes

  • Light jacket or fleece for evenings
  • Umbrella/rain shell, especially Jun‚ÄìOct
  • Comfortable walking shoes

Getting There & Getting Around

Baguio is well-connected by bus from Metro Manila and nearby provinces. Within the city, taxis and jeepneys are widely available, and many attractions are walkable.

By Bus

Most popular
  • From Metro Manila: Victory Liner, Genesis JoyBus, Solid North. Travel time 4.5‚Äì6.5 hrs depending on traffic.
  • Terminals in Baguio: Gov. Pack Rd (near Session Rd) and new terminals along Marcos Highway area.
  • Tip: Book premium/non-stop coaches for comfort; bring a light jacket for aircon coaches.

By Private Car

  • Routes: Kennon Rd, Marcos Hwy, or Naguilian Rd (Marcos is most stable in rainy season).
  • Expect steep, winding roads. Check weather and road advisories before driving.
  • Parking in the city center is limited; consider paid parking buildings.

Taxis & Ride-hailing

  • Taxis are metered and generally honest. Flag down rate is affordable for short hops.
  • Ride-hailing availability varies by time/day; have a backup (taxi or jeepney).
  • Always ask for a receipt if available; keep small bills ready.

Jeepneys & Walking

  • Jeepneys follow fixed routes. Pay upon boarding; prepare exact fare if possible.
  • City center is walkable but hilly. Wear comfortable shoes and watch for steep steps.
  • Session Road, Burnham Park, and nearby attractions are best explored on foot.

Local Tips

Respect Nature

Stick to trails, don‚Äôt pick flowers or pine cones, and dispose of trash properly.

Cash & ATMs

Cards are accepted in many cafes, but markets and small shops are cash-preferred.

Connectivity

4G is common in the city center; it may drop in valleys or forested areas.
